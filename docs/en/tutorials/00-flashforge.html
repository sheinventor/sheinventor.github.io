Check Printer Status

Make sure the printer is clean, with no filament residue on the nozzle or the print bed.

Clean the print bed with a paper towel and isopropyl alcohol.

How to Turn On the Printer

Ensure the printer is connected to a power socket.

Press the power button located at the back, then the one at the front.

Wait for the system to fully initialize before continuing.

The printer is on when this screen appears.

How to Load Filament (If No Filament Is Present)

Place the filament at the back of the printer.

Open the top door.

Place the end of the filament in the white tube and push until it comes out on the other side.

Go to the menu via the touch screen and select the ‚ÄúFilament‚Äù option.

Choose the ‚ÄúChange Filament‚Äù option.

Wait until the temperature reaches 220 ¬∞C

Insert the filament into the extruder as shown in the image. If done correctly, the printer will detect it and start extruding.

The printer will extrude filament for a while to clean out any remnants of the previous filament.

Remove the printed filament.

How to Change the Filament (by Removing the Old Filament):

Open the top door.

Remove the white tube and cut the old filament with pliers.

Note: Pliers are available next to the printers. If not, ask the FabManagers.

Remove the filament from the white tube by gently pulling with the spool, then secure its end to prevent it from unwinding.

Place the new filament on the base at the back of the printer.

Place the end of the filament in the white tube and push until it comes out on the other side.

Go to the menu via the touch screen and select the ‚ÄúFilament‚Äù option.

Choose the ‚ÄúChange Filament‚Äù option and ‚ÄúStart.‚Äù

Wait until the temperature reaches 220 ¬∞C.

Insert the filament into the extruder as shown in the image. If done correctly, the printer will detect it and start extruding.

The printer will extrude for a moment to clean out the previous filament. Check the extrusion; if necessary, restart the process.

Remove the printed filament.

Removing the Model

Once the print is complete, wait for the bed to cool down to avoid damage.

Grab the gray handle of the print bed and apply slight pressure upwards.

While holding the plate by two sides, twist it slightly to detach the print. If necessary, rotate it 90 degrees and repeat on the other sides.
